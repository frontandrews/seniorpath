# Article Structure Template

Use this as the default structure for new SeniorPath articles in both `pt-br` and `en`.

The structure is editorial guidance. It is not a UI component contract.

## Required visible sections

```md
## The problem

## Mental model

## Breaking it down

## Simple example

## Common mistakes

## How a senior thinks

## What the interviewer wants to see
```

## Required ending

End the article with one or two short blockquotes.

Do not render labels like:

- `Final mandatory`
- `Direct summary`
- `Anti-bullshit phrase`

Those are internal writing concepts, not visible headings.

Use this shape instead:

```md
> One sentence that closes the concept clearly.

> Optional second sentence with the strongest product-style takeaway.
```

## Tone rules

- Write like you are explaining to someone smart who does not want fluff.
- Prefer plain words over impressive words.
- Use one concrete example before abstract advice.
- Make trade-offs explicit.
- Keep paragraphs short.
- If a sentence sounds like performance, simplify it.

## Product voice

SeniorPath content should feel:

- direct
- calm
- useful on first read
- interview-relevant
- anti-bullshit

## Notes

- `title` is the article promise.
- `description` is the subtitle shown on cards and article headers.
- `summary` can exist in frontmatter, but it should not replace `description` in article cards or next reads.
